# C10.5 Big Mold Chase – Cavity Damage During Disassembly

## Detail Information
- Detail information: 00:55 – 01:15
- EQ Owner: C10.5 Big mold chase
- Issue happened time & date: 01:15 2025/06/05
- Mold chase ID: P85136053
- Cavity damaged ID: 18224264101A

## Symptom
- Cavity was damaged during disassembly from mold chase.
- The cavity could not be removed normally.
- Screw and hammer were used during removal and damage occurred.

## Issue Finding
- Use the screw to remove cavity from mold chase led to damage.
- Mold chase temperature was 175°C.
- Because of temperature expansion between mold chase and cavity, the parts became tight and could not be removed normally.
- The team used 2 screws to secure and used a hammer to push cavity out of mold chase.
- However, 4 screws in the venting position had still not been removed.
- Hammering while the cavity was still retained by 4 screws led to cavity damage.

## Root Cause
- Different removal method exists between C10.5 Small mold chase and C10.5 Big mold chase.
- C10.5 Small mold chase includes 5 parts with separate venting (position 1 & 5).
- For C10.5 Small, venting should be kept and removed last when removing cavity from mold chase.
- C10.5 Big mold chase includes 3 parts with attached venting.
- For C10.5 Big, all screws behind mold chase must be removed before removing cavity.
- The same method of C10.5 Small was incorrectly used for C10.5 Big.
- Because of this wrong method, the cavity was damaged.

## Failure Mechanism
- At 175°C, thermal expansion reduced clearance between mold chase and cavity.
- Mechanical separation force was then applied using screws and hammer.
- Because 4 venting-position screws were still holding the cavity, the cavity was not actually free.
- Hammer impact while the cavity was still mechanically retained created localized damage.
- The failure was caused by incorrect disassembly method under hot and tight-fit condition, not by normal wear alone.
