# C10.5 Small Mold Chase – Venting Damaged During Assembly

## Issue Summary
- EQ Owner: C10.5 Small mold chase
- Issue happened time & date: 18:30 26/02/2026
- Equipment recovery timeline in slide:
  - 26/02 18:00 → Take C10.5 Small mold chase to assembly after cleaning by ultrasonic machine
  - 26/02 18:30 → Use the screw to assemble venting, which led to damage

## Symptom
- Venting was damaged during mold-chase assembly.
- Damage occurred while using screws to assemble the venting component.

## Issue Finding
- Use the screw to assemble venting cavity led to damage.
- C10.5 Small venting position was shown in the slide.
- Mold chase base thickness: 11.2 mm
- Venting seat depth: 4.5 mm
- Total depth: 11.2 + 4.5 = 15.7 mm
- M4x16 mm screws were used to assemble the venting.
- When tightening the screw, the screw bottomed out at the end of the threaded hole.
- That bottom-out condition applied force to the venting at the thinnest area.

## Root Cause
- The screw length was too long for the actual venting assembly stack-up.
- M4x16 mm screws bottomed out before proper tightening was achieved.
- Bottom-out caused concentrated force on the thinnest venting section and damaged the venting.

## Failure Mechanism
- The mold chase base thickness plus venting seat depth created a total stack condition of 15.7 mm.
- With M4x16 mm screws, the screw reached the end of the threaded hole during tightening.
- Because the screw bottomed out, tightening force was transferred abnormally into the venting part instead of creating proper clamping.
- The venting’s thinnest area absorbed that force and was damaged.

## Corrective Action
1. Replace the existing M4x16 mm screws with M4x14 mm screws for this mold chase.
2. Apply the same screw-length correction to other mold chases to prevent recurrence.
3. Slide status: done.

## Preventive Action
1. Contact the vendor to investigate why M4x16 mm screws were originally used for venting assembly.
2. Keep the corrected screw specification controlled for future assembly and maintenance.

## Current Status
- Corrective replacement from M4x16 mm to M4x14 mm was completed.
- Improvement is planned to be applied to other mold chases.
- Vendor investigation remains pending.

## Impact
- Quality: venting component damaged during assembly
- Production: mold chase assembly / readiness impacted
- Safety: mechanical assembly force misapplication risk
- Repeat risk: possible if wrong screw length is used again

## Open Risk / Follow-up
- Need vendor feedback on original screw specification
- Need to confirm all similar mold chases are checked and corrected
- Need to control spare-part screw specification to avoid mixing M4x16 and M4x14 in future assembly
